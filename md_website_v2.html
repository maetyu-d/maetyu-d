ABOUT
Artist-Designer · Researcher · Educator
I'm interested in experimental audiovisual interfaces, creative coding (particularly procedural generation), modular synthesizers, and DIY/DIWO approaches to disability and technology.
WORK
  • SL in Game Audio and Technical Design, University of Staffordshire 2023—present
  • Trustee of the The OHMI Trust 2024—present
  • Research: Accessible musical instruments and interfaces, audiovisual systems (design and history), game audio (procedural audio), disability and technology.
  • Teaching: Game audio (audio programming, procedural audio, generative music, sound synthesis and DSP), film sound and audiovisual theory (Chion, Altman, etc.), microcontrollers and embedded computing, game design, project management, and UG and PG project and PhD supervision.
SELECTED PROJECTS
  • limp.freq (2025) — symmetric bots that draw connections between mobility impairment—specifically my own experience of leg length discrepancy (LLD)—and its impact on daily movement, navigation, and spatial needs, while also challenging ideas of perfection that have been baked into AI since its earliest days.
  • Procedural Audio Objects (2021) — with Sarah Whitfield. Two practical pieces (a prop and a generative radio play based on Interior, Maeterlinck's 1895 play in rhymed dialogue, and housed in a tactile, portable object) that explore the possibilities of sound in theatre and generative audio in particular.
  • Beyond Audio Description for Theatre (2017-2019) — an ERAS-funded project that explored the use of ambiently-diffused sound design as supplement to and replacement for audio description for theatre.
  • Postrum (2014-2015) — with Steve Spencer. A system that uses a 3D camera to track the posture of trumpet students, paired with a haptic belt that gently corrects deviations which could otherwise increase the risk of long-term injuries or musculoskeletal problems.
  • The Haptic Drum Kit/Haptic Bracelets (2009-2012) — with Simon Holland and Anders Bouwer. Wearable systems that send haptic impulses to the user's wrists and ankles, giving guidance on how to play rhythm patterns, particularly multi-limb patterns.
